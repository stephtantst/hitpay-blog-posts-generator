How this tool works
What it does

HitPay Blog Generator is an internal content tool for the marketing team. It uses Claude AI to produce SEO- and AEO-optimised, market-accurate blog posts for Southeast Asia — then manages the full editorial workflow from draft to publication.

Posts are written for three markets: 🇸🇬 SG 🇲🇾 MY 🇵🇭 PH — or all three at once if no market is selected. You can generate a post from a keyword, or rewrite an existing post by URL. Finished posts export as CSV for Framer CMS.

Two creation modes
✦
Generate New Post — enter a keyword or topic. The AI researches, structures, and writes a full post from scratch with all optimisations applied. Tagged New in the post list.
↺
Rewrite Existing Post — paste any blog post URL. The AI scrapes the existing content, then rewrites it with improved structure, SEO, AEO optimisation, and updated HitPay positioning. The source URL is stored and shown in the editor sidebar. Tagged Rewrite.
Data sources used during generation
1
HitPay MCP server — queries the internal knowledge base across general product guide categories (up to 11 results). Grounds the AI in accurate HitPay features, flows, and merchant use cases.
2
Product documentation — hitpay_docs.md is split into sections and scored by keyword relevance. The top sections (up to 30,000 chars) are injected for accurate feature names, flows, and pricing.
3
Competitor research database — competitors/*.json profiles payment competitors by features, markets, and weaknesses. Relevant profiles are matched by keyword and market for positioning context.
4
Internal blog link index — blog_links.yaml lists all published HitPay posts by topic. The AI picks exactly 3 genuinely relevant URLs and weaves them as natural in-content hyperlinks — never as a list.
What the AI is instructed to do

Every post is generated with a detailed system prompt. Key rules:

  • Lead with the reader's problem — not HitPay's features. Product gets mentioned in 1–2 sections at most.
  • 900–1,200 words, 3–5 H2 sections, sparingly-used H3s. No H1 (CMS adds it).
  • No marketing language — "seamlessly", "revolutionise", "game-changer", "cutting-edge" are explicitly banned.
  • Market-accurate payment methods — per-market lists of valid methods, plus a fact-check list of cross-market misattributions to avoid (e.g. PayNow is SG-only).
  • Local specificity — district and city names required per market (Tanjong Pagar, Bangsar, BGC).
  • No fabricated stats or testimonials — all claims must come from the knowledge base.
  • Exactly 3 internal backlinks woven naturally using descriptive anchor text — 3 well-placed relevant links outperform 5 forced ones for GEO citability.
  • Payout messaging — always "next business day in SG, MY & PH", never just "T+1".
  • Competitor comparisons — factual, non-disparaging. Acknowledge competitor strengths, then show where HitPay wins on relevant dimensions (fees, ease of onboarding, payout speed, local support). No fake data.
AEO — AI Answer Engine Optimisation

Every post is structured to rank in AI-generated answers (Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude). Rules applied automatically:

  • Direct answer in the first paragraph — answers the core question within 40–60 words so AI engines can extract it as a snippet.
  • H2/H3 headings as questions — "What is…", "How does…", "Which… is best for…" — the format AI engines use to pull FAQ-style answers.
  • Mandatory FAQ section — 4–6 Q&A pairs at the end, each with a concise 2–3 sentence answer.
  • Numbered and bulleted lists — for any step-by-step processes or comparisons, improving extractability.
  • Comparison tables — when comparing HitPay to alternatives, uses Markdown tables AI engines can parse.
  • Cited data points — real numbers (fees, limits, timelines) stated plainly so they can be quoted.
  • [SCHEMA] block — each post includes a JSON-LD schema block (FAQPage or HowTo) for structured data markup.
Generation pipeline (in order)
1
Query HitPay MCP knowledge base (general + product + guide)
2
Score and inject relevant doc sections from hitpay_docs.md
3
Match competitor profiles by keyword + market from competitors/*.json
4
Build internal backlink reference from blog_links.yaml and select 3 external links using the GEO authority strategy (see below)
5
Rewrite only: scrape the source URL, extract title and body text
6
Send all context to claude-opus with the full system prompt (SEO + AEO + competitor rules)
7
Parse JSON response → save to Supabase + open in editor
GEO link strategy — why articles are linked the way they are

GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) is about getting HitPay content cited by AI answer engines — Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Claude. AI engines evaluate outbound links as a trust signal: linking to authoritative, diverse sources makes content more citable. Linking to the same partner blog every article signals shallow sourcing and reduces citability.

Internal backlinks: 3 (not 5)

Three highly relevant internal links are better than five padded ones. AI engines evaluate anchor text relevance — a forced link with weak context actively lowers the article's authority signal. The AI is instructed to only link where it is genuinely relevant, never to hit a number.

External links: always 3, fixed slot roles

1
Regulator (always) — MAS for SG, Bank Negara Malaysia for MY, BSP for PH. Government and central bank URLs are the strongest authority signal available. This slot is never skipped.
2
Official source — the most keyword-relevant payment method page (e.g. PayNow, DuitNow, GCash official pages) or, for integration articles, the relevant platform's documentation. First-party official pages, not blog posts.
3
Research / data source — rotates deterministically by keyword across: Singapore Department of Statistics, PayNet Malaysia, Philippine Statistics Authority, Statista market reports, World Bank financial inclusion data, Google-Temasek e-Conomy SEA. This ensures no two articles cite the same research source, which AI engines read as breadth of evidence.

Comparison articles — slots 2 and 3 are filled with competitor blog articles (one per competitor, from the scraped article database), so competitor links appear with specific context rather than just a homepage. All competitor links use rel="nofollow" and must only appear in sentences directly about that specific competitor.

Competitor links appear no earlier than 30% into the article. Placing a competitor link in the intro or first section causes readers to click away before they've engaged with the HitPay article. The AI is instructed to place all competitor links in the second half of the body — inside comparison or context sections — never in the opening.

Why not Shopify and WooCommerce blog posts? Partner blog articles are weak GEO signals — AI engines interpret repeated links to the same partner blog as affiliate bias, not genuine research. Shopify and WooCommerce only appear as external links when the article is specifically about Shopify or WooCommerce integration (slot 2, official docs URL only — not a blog post).

Editorial workflow
  • ✨ Generated — freshly generated, ready for review.
  • ✏️ Editing — in progress, being edited before approval.
  • 🔵 Ready to Publish — reviewed and approved for publishing.
  • ✅ Published — live on the blog.

The Lifecycle section in each post's sidebar records when it moved through each stage, and shows elapsed time between steps — so the team can track content pipeline speed.

Use AI Edit to send a selected passage (or the full post) back to Claude with a specific instruction, without regenerating the whole article.
