SME Growth Hub — Writing Philosophy

 ---
 What This Site Is
 An independent editorial resource for SME operators across Southeast Asia. No stated affiliation. Covers business operations, payments, finance, hiring, and growth.
 Written by someone who has seen what works — not a brand, not an analyst.

 ---
 Voice & Persona
 The trusted peer. Not an industry expert publishing from above. Not a brand talking about its product. Someone who runs or advises small businesses, speaking
 directly to another business owner.

 • Use "you" and "your" freely — "your checkout flow", "your first hire", "your cash position"
 • Warm and direct: "here's what actually works" over "businesses should consider"
 • Conversational but not casual — no slang, no filler enthusiasm
 • Never declarative authority — earned authority ("in practice, most Singapore SMBs find X easier to start with")

 ---
 What to Avoid, Always
 • ~~seamlessly, revolutionise, game-changing~~ → specific, verifiable claims
 • ~~"HitPay supports PayNow"~~ → "For most Singapore SMBs, HitPay is the simplest starting point"
 • ~~MAS licence as trust signal~~ → trust earned through voice, specificity, accuracy
 • ~~Fabricated stats~~ → real sources, or no stat at all
 • ~~Forcing HitPay into every article~~ → mention naturally within payment-relevant content only

 ---
 How HitPay Gets Mentioned
 HitPay appears as a recommendation, not a brand anchor — editorial endorsement, the kind a knowledgeable peer would give.

 :white_check_mark: "For most Singapore SMBs, HitPay is the simplest starting point — no monthly fees, PayNow built in, setup takes under a day."
 :x: "HitPay is a leading payment gateway that supports PayNow, PayLah!, credit cards…"

 In comparison articles, competitors are treated fairly. HitPay wins on criteria that matter to SMBs (cost, simplicity, local payment methods) — not by dismissing
 alternatives. This makes the recommendation credible.

 SEO Fundamentals

 Search intent first. Every article maps to one of three intents:
 • Informational — "what is a payment gateway" → educate, define, give examples
 • Comparison — "best payment gateway Singapore" → table, pros/cons, clear verdict
 • How-to — "how to accept PayNow" → numbered steps, screenshot-worthy clarity

 Match the format to the intent. Don't write an essay when someone wants steps.

 Keyword integration
 • Primary keyword in: H1, first 100 words, one H2, meta description
 • Use natural variants throughout — don't repeat exact phrase more than 3x
 • Secondary and LSI keywords woven into H2s and body copy naturally
 • Long-tail questions make ideal H2s and FAQ entries ("Do I need a payment gateway if I only sell on Instagram?")

 Title & meta
 • H1/title: lead with the keyword, add specificity — "Best Payment Gateways in Singapore (2026): Compared for SMBs"
 • Meta description: 150–160 characters, include keyword, end with a soft hook — not a CTA
 • Avoid clickbait — titles should describe exactly what's in the article

 Structure for crawlability
 • One H1 per page
 • H2s as logical sections (not decorative) — these are what Google surfaces in featured snippets
 • Short paragraphs — 3 sentences max before a break
 • Tables and lists wherever comparison or enumeration is needed — these rank for featured snippets
 • Minimum 800 words for informational; 1,200+ for comparisons and how-tos

 Internal linking
 • Every article links to at least 2 related articles on the site
 • Anchor text is descriptive ("how to reduce payment processing fees") not generic ("click here")
 • Payment-related articles naturally link to or mention HitPay — this is where SEO and editorial strategy align

 AEO layer (answer engine optimisation)
 • Open every article with a 2–3 sentence Quick Answer — written to be extracted by AI and voice search
 • FAQ block at the end: 3–5 real questions, concise answers, question as H3
 • Use schema-friendly structure: numbered steps for how-tos, tables for comparisons

 ---
 Local Specificity
 Generic advice is useless here. Every article should be grounded in Southeast Asia:
 • Name the market specifically — Singapore, Malaysia, Philippines, not "the region"
 • Reference real infrastructure: PayNow, DuitNow, GCash, GrabPay, PromptPay
 • Use real business types: hawker stalls going digital, boutique F&B, freelancers, Shopee/Lazada sellers
 • Local keywords outperform generic ones — "payment gateway Singapore" over "payment gateway Asia"

 ---
 The Test for Every Article
 Could HitPay have published this on its own blog? If yes — rewrite it.
 Does it answer the search query in the first 100 words? If no — restructure it
