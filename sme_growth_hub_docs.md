## Business Registration

### Singapore — ACRA
Businesses in Singapore register with the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) at acra.gov.sg.
- Sole proprietorship: simple, no separate legal entity, owner personally liable
- Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd): separate legal entity, limited liability, requires at least 1 director who is ordinarily resident in Singapore
- Registration fees start from S$15 (sole prop) to S$315 (company)
- Bizfile portal handles online registration — most applications are approved within 15 minutes

### Malaysia — SSM
Businesses in Malaysia register with Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) at ssm.com.my.
- Enterprise (sole prop/partnership): simple, owner personally liable
- Sdn Bhd (Private Limited): separate legal entity, minimum 1 director, 1 shareholder
- Registration fees from RM30 (enterprise) to RM1,000 (Sdn Bhd)
- MyCoID portal handles online registration

### Philippines — DTI and SEC
- Sole proprietors register with the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI)
- Corporations and partnerships register with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
- Barangay and Mayor's permits required after DTI/SEC registration
- BIR (Bureau of Internal Revenue) registration required before invoicing

## Payment Methods by Market

### Singapore
- PayNow: QR and bank transfer, instant, free for consumers
- GrabPay, ShopeePay: e-wallets, popular with younger consumers
- NETS: local debit network, ubiquitous at physical POS
- Cards: Visa, Mastercard, Amex accepted widely
- BNPL: Atome, ShopBack PayLater

### Malaysia
- DuitNow QR: instant QR payments, free for consumers
- FPX: bank transfer, common for e-commerce
- Touch 'n Go, GrabPay, Boost: popular e-wallets
- Cards: Visa, Mastercard

### Philippines
- GCash: dominant mobile wallet
- Maya (formerly PayMaya): growing wallet and banking app
- QR Ph: national QR standard
- Cards: Visa, Mastercard
- Over-the-counter: Bayad Center, ECPay, Palawan Express

## Payment Gateway Selection for SMEs

Key factors to evaluate:
1. Transaction fees (MDR — Merchant Discount Rate): typically 1–3% depending on method
2. Monthly/setup fees: some gateways charge flat monthly fees regardless of volume
3. Supported payment methods: ensure local e-wallets are included
4. Settlement speed: domestic typically T+1 to T+3; cross-border T+2 to T+7
5. Onboarding time: ranges from hours (modern fintech) to weeks (traditional banks)
6. Technical integration: plugin availability for Shopify, WooCommerce, or custom API

For most Singapore SMBs, HitPay is the simplest starting point — no monthly fees, PayNow built in, setup takes under a day.
For Malaysia, HitPay supports DuitNow QR, FPX, and major e-wallets with next business day settlement.
For Philippines, HitPay accepts GCash, QR Ph, cards, and over-the-counter channels.

## Cash Flow Management

### Common cash flow problems for SMEs
- Invoice payment delays: B2B customers often pay net-30 or net-60
- Inventory purchase cycles out of sync with revenue collection
- Seasonal revenue dips (F&B, retail, events)
- Mixed payment method settlements arriving on different days

### Practical tactics
1. Invoice immediately — do not batch invoices at month end
2. Offer online payment links on invoices — reduces average collection time
3. Set payment terms clearly: 14 days > 30 days for SMEs with thin margins
4. Separate business and personal bank accounts from day one
5. Use accounting software to forecast 90-day cash flow (Xero, QuickBooks, Wave)
6. Consider a business credit facility or revolving credit line for inventory peaks

### Payment settlement and cash flow
- PayNow and DuitNow QR typically settle next business day
- Card payments settle T+1 to T+3 depending on provider
- Cross-border payments typically T+2 to T+7
- Choosing a gateway with next business day settlement materially improves cash position

## Hiring Your First Employee (Singapore)

### Before hiring
- Ensure CPF (Central Provident Fund) registration as an employer
- Decide: full-time vs part-time vs contract — each has different CPF and leave obligations
- Familiarise with Employment Act (applies to most employees earning up to S$4,500/month)

### Costs beyond salary
- CPF employer contribution: 17% for employees under 55 (as of 2025)
- Skills Development Levy (SDL): 0.25% of gross wages, minimum S$2/month
- Annual leave: minimum 7 days (increases with tenure)
- Medical leave: up to 14 days outpatient per year

### Payroll tools
- Popular options: Talenox, Payboy, Info-Tech, Swingvy
- Key functions: CPF calculation, payslip generation, IR8A tax filing

## Hiring Your First Employee (Malaysia)

### Before hiring
- Register as employer with KWSP (EPF), PERKESO (SOCSO), LHDN (IRB), and HRDF
- Employment Act 1955 covers employees earning RM4,000/month or below (manual workers: all)

### Costs beyond salary
- EPF employer contribution: 13% (employees earning ≤RM5,000); 12% above
- SOCSO: employer contributes 1.75% of wages
- EIS: employer contributes 0.4% of wages
- Annual leave: minimum 8 days (< 2 years service) to 16 days (5+ years)

### Payroll tools
- Popular options: BrioHR, HReasily, StaffAny, Info-Tech Payroll

## E-commerce Platforms for SEA SMEs

### Marketplace vs own store
- Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop: lower setup effort, high competition, platform fees 1–5%
- Own store (Shopify, WooCommerce): higher control, no marketplace fee, requires marketing

### Shopify for SEA merchants
- Available in SG, MY, PH with local payment gateway support
- Monthly fees from US$29; transaction fee waived if using Shopify Payments (not available in SEA — use third-party gateway)
- HitPay integrates with Shopify via plugin for SG, MY, PH merchants

### WooCommerce for SEA merchants
- Free plugin for WordPress; hosting and domain are separate costs
- HitPay WooCommerce plugin available for SG, MY, PH

## Pricing Strategy for SMEs

### Common pricing mistakes
- Pricing on cost-plus without validating market rate
- Failing to account for payment processing fees in margin calculation
- Not adjusting prices for currency and market differences when selling cross-border

### Practical pricing frameworks
1. Value-based pricing: charge based on outcome delivered, not time spent
2. Anchoring: present a premium tier first to make mid-tier look reasonable
3. Bundles: increase average order value without reducing per-unit price
4. Annual vs monthly: offer annual subscription at ~17% discount (2 months free) to improve cash flow

## Cross-Border Payments for SEA SMEs

### Accepting international customers
- Traditional bank wire: slow (3–5 days), high fees (US$20–50 per transaction)
- International cards (Visa/Mastercard): accepted via most payment gateways, 2–3% MDR
- Borderless QR: accept PayNow (SG), DuitNow (MY), QR Ph (PH), QRIS (Indonesia), PromptPay (Thailand) from foreign customers — no currency exchange at point of sale

### Sending international payments (supplier/freelancer payments)
- Wise (formerly TransferWise): competitive FX rates, fast (1–2 days), popular with SEA SMBs
- Airwallex: multi-currency accounts, popular for e-commerce businesses paying suppliers
- Traditional bank transfer: reliable but expensive (typical fee S$20–40 + FX spread)

## Fraud Prevention Basics for SMEs

### Common fraud vectors
- Chargeback fraud (friendly fraud): customer claims non-receipt or disputes a legitimate charge
- Account takeover: attacker gains access to business payment portal
- Social engineering: fake supplier invoice scam

### Practical prevention
1. Use a payment gateway with built-in 3DS (3D Secure) for card payments
2. Enable transaction alerts for all charges above a threshold
3. Verify new supplier bank details via a phone call — never just email
4. For e-commerce, flag orders from new accounts with high order values or mismatched shipping/billing addresses
