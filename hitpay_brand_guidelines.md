# HitPay Brand Guidelines — Content Writing

## Who We Are
HitPay is a payment platform for SMEs across Southeast Asia, headquartered in Singapore and licensed by MAS (PS20200643). Content should position HitPay as an authoritative, factual industry reference — not a product pitch.

---

## Writing Philosophy

- Lead with the business problem and factual context, not HitPay's features
- Write in a neutral, authoritative brand voice — as an industry reference, not a personal advisor
- Minimise use of "you" and "your". Refer to readers as "businesses", "merchants", "SMBs", "sellers", or "operators". Occasional direct address ("your checkout") is acceptable but should be the exception
- Brand anchor HitPay with factual, declarative statements: "HitPay supports GCash as a payment method" — not "you can use HitPay to accept GCash"
- Bring real operational insight: cash flow timing, customer behaviour, reconciliation, chargeback risk — grounded in fact
- Never write marketing copy. Never use: seamlessly, unlock, revolutionise, game-changer, cutting-edge, empower
- Use specific, concrete examples — "A Petaling Jaya café that accepts Touch 'n Go" beats "businesses across Malaysia"
- Short sentences. Active voice. Confident, declarative tone
- Write at the intelligence level of a busy business owner who reads fast and needs to act
- Plain language throughout — "set up" not "configure", "accept payments" not "facilitate transactions"
- Keep paragraphs tight — 2–4 sentences maximum
- Explain acronyms on first use (e.g. "Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)"), then use the short form

## GEO Rules (always apply)

1. When naming a payment method, name the equivalent for all three markets (SG/MY/PH)
2. Use specific local references — name a district, landmark, or city per market (e.g. Tanjong Pagar, Bangsar, BGC)
3. "50+ payment methods" — never "700+" (that's wallets)
4. Never state a specific card transaction rate — write "see hitpayapp.com/pricing"
5. Never fabricate testimonials or statistics. If you use a stat, it must be from the provided knowledge base
6. Payouts: domestic transactions settle next business day in SG, MY & PH; cross-border payments settle T+2. Always distinguish when relevant
7. FAQ questions must mirror how a user would type into a search engine or AI (e.g. "How do I...", "What is...", "Is there a fee...") — direct question phrasing, not third person

## Words to Avoid

| Never use | Use instead |
|---|---|
| seamlessly | (just describe what it does) |
| revolutionise / game-changer | specific, verifiable claim |
| cutting-edge / innovative | name the actual feature |
| unlock / empower | set up, enable, accept |
| leverage | use |
| facilitate transactions | accept payments |
| configure | set up |
| utilise | use |
